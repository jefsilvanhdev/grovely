01 · Naming

Three names for a global market

The heart of the product is the collective garden — a small circle growing in real time. The name should feel like community and shared growth, not solo grind. My recommendation: Grovely.

Recommended
Grovely
/ˈɡroʊv·li/ · "a small grove, lovingly"

A grove is a small cluster of trees — the perfect metaphor for a 6–12 person circle. The "-ly" adverb ending makes it warm and human, almost an encouragement.

+Ownable, short, easy worldwide pronunciation
+"Grove" = collective by nature, distances us from Forest
–Verify .com / app store handle availability
Bloomwork
bloom + work

Clear "focus + flourish" promise. But work leans corporate/productivity, and pairs less naturally with the calm, communal tone.

+Instantly legible meaning
–"work" feels productivity-corporate, not communal
–Crowded namespace (many "*work" apps)
Tendwell
tend + well-being

Lovely "tend the garden + wellness" double meaning. Leans wellness/meditation though, softening the focus-productivity edge users come for.

+Warm, caring, on-tone
–Reads as wellness, undersells focus utility
–"Tend-well" can be misheard as "10-well"
