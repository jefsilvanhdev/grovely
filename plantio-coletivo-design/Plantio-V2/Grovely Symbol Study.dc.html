Mark exploration

Growth, built into the mark

The grove already says "many trees." These six directions push further — making the symbol itself express a group growing together, not just standing together. Each is drawn in one 100×100 grid so it drops straight into the app icon.

Recommended
Ascending grove

Three trees on a shared mound, each a little taller than the last — the group at different stages, all rooted in the same ground. It keeps the grove DNA but adds an unmistakable upward, growing rhythm. The animation shows what it's really about: each member rises in turn, the sun arrives when they're grown.

Benchmarking

How the category signs growth

The space is crowded with one idea: a single tree that grows while you focus. Forest gamifies productivity by growing a virtual tree as you stay on task, and the tree withers if you leave the app. Flora uses the same grow-a-plant idea and adds a "grow trees with friends" feature for group focus. Almost everyone signs growth the same four ways:

Single tree
Forest · Flora · Treecard
Literal & warm, instantly understood. But it says solo, and the lane is bumper-to-bumper.
Mascot / character
Habitica · Finch · Study Bunny
Habitica turns life into a game where a character progresses as your routines improve. Charming, but costly to build and off-tone for calm focus.
Streak rings
Loop · minimalist trackers
Simple circles fill as streaks grow. Clean and calm, but generic and emotionally cold — could be any tracker.
People cluster
Cohorty · HabitShare
Cohorty centers small cohorts of 5–15 people doing a challenge together. Says "group" — but as abstract dots, with no growth and no warmth.
Where the white space is
Nobody owns collective + living growth — many plants read as one warm, growing system. That's Grovely's lane.

The read: stay botanical and warm (unlike rings & dots), but make the mark inherently plural and growing (unlike the single tree). Every direction below is engineered to live in that lower-right corner.

More suggestions

Five more, aimed at the open corner

Each takes a different route to "collective + living." My new favorite is Constellation grove — it says connected group out loud while staying a grove, a clean complement to the Ascending grove.

Constellation grove New pick
Trees linked like a constellation. The line is the relationship — group, said warmly.
Venn grove
Three canopies overlapping into a shared core. The most ownable, abstract take on "what we grow together".
Heart canopy
Two trees lean in; their crowns form a green heart. Warmest of all — watch it doesn't get too sweet.
Plus-leaf
A leaf built on a plus — "add a friend, grow." The most productivity-forward; least obviously a grove.
Garden badge
A ring holds a little grove — "our shared plot". Fills an app tile beautifully; reads as a community emblem.
