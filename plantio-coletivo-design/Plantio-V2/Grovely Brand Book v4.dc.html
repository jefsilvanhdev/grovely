01 · Naming

The name

The heart of the product is the collective garden — a small circle growing in real time. The name should feel like community and shared growth, not solo grind. The name we landed on: Grovely.

Grovely
/ˈɡroʊv·li/ · "a small grove, lovingly"

A grove is a small cluster of trees — the perfect metaphor for a 6–12 person circle. The "-ly" adverb ending makes it warm and human, almost an encouragement.

+Ownable, short, easy worldwide pronunciation
+"Grove" = collective by nature, distances us from Forest

03 · Symbol & app icon

A little grove

The v1 radial mark read as a flower — concept right, legibility wrong. v2 went literal with three rounded trees, but the crowns read as lollipops. v4 commits to conifers: three pines in rising heights, so the silhouette is unmistakably a tree at any size. The cluster still implies the circle of friends, and a single sun keeps it warm. Bold, tiered shape holds down to 32px.

04 · Growing together

Showing growth in a group

The app icon stays the grove. But across the product — loading states, the collective screen, share cards — we need a motif for many people growing as one. The defined motif: shared roots — separate trees of different heights, one connected root system.

Collective motif
Shared roots

Separate trees of different heights — each person at their own pace — drawing from one connected root system. The togetherness lives underground, exactly like a real grove (a stand of aspens is one organism). Honest about individual progress, united at the base.

07 · Illustration

A garden you can grow

Every plant is drawn in one style — organic soft: layered green tones, a soft highlight and a tapered trunk make each tree feel a little more real, while staying friendly and flat-shaded (no gradients).

{{ styleRow }}

The library — 5 plants + 1 seasonal

Each grows through six levels — seed, sprout, sapling, young, mature, elder — plus a withered state for a failed session. A rare seventh level, Centenarian, is reserved for species famed for ancient giants — oak and pine (think sequoia). Same shape language, only the canopy scales and fills in.

07b · Seasonal & event skins

Dress the grove for the season

One tree, many moods. A theme skin recolors the canopy and layers light decorations on top of any plant or stage — a reason to come back, and a natural premium hook. Same silhouette underneath, so nothing breaks.

{{ themeRow }}

Skins are a tint set + an overlay layer — cheap to add. Seasonal ones rotate automatically; event ones unlock for the moment (a World Cup, a holiday) and live in the user's collection after.
